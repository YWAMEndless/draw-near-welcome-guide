Remembering Room

Welcome

There are many ways to remember someone we love.

The Remembering Room is an opportunity to slow down together. Through storytelling, photographs, handwritten notes, and creativity, each person contributes one page that becomes part of a shared memory book for the family.

Two mental health professionals will facilitate a time to gather, create, and share.

There is no artistic ability required.
There is no right way to remember.

Simply come as you are.
Before you arrive

How to Prepare

Each participant will fill in one to two pages
to contribute to the memory book.

Look through these prompts and pick one or two that resonate with you. Take some time before our event to journal, collect pictures, and reflect on how you will fill your page(s). These questions are a starting point to help foster inspiration. Don’t try to answer them all — just one is enough.

  1. Write down words or phrases they used all the time — we will turn these into quotes.
  2. What were some of their fondest quirks, habits, or daily routines?
  3. How would you describe their laugh or sense of humor?
  4. What is your earliest memory of them?
  5. Can you share a specific story that captures something essential about their personality?
  6. Did they have favorite stories they loved to tell — from childhood, their teen years, early adulthood, or later in life?
  7. What were the biggest hardships or challenges they faced, and how did they approach or overcome them?
  8. How did they show love or care — for others, and for you personally?
  9. What was something they taught you, either on purpose or without realizing it?
  10. What causes, hobbies, or passions were they most proud of?
  11. What do you miss the most about them?
  12. What is the image of them that most often comes to mind?
  13. When people think of them, how would they want to be remembered?
A note on photos

Please only bring photographs — or copies of photographs — that you feel comfortable cutting or leaving in the book. What you bring will stay with the family.

If a specific item reminds you of your loved one, please take a picture of it and bring the photo instead of the item, so we can include it in the book.

Don’t worry if you don’t have any of these.
Your memories are enough.

Preparing Your Home

A little preparation makes the room feel gracious.
Here is all you need —

  • One large or multiple tables, if possible
  • Good lighting
  • Light snacks
  • Coffee or tea
  • Soft instrumental music
  • A nearby place for photos and keepsakes
  • Tissues
  • Enough chairs for everyone
We will take care of the rest.
