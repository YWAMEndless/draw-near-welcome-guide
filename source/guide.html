The Remembering Room
Welcome

There are many ways
to remember someone we love.

Some memories make us laugh.
Some bring tears.
Some are stories we’ve told a hundred times.
Others have never been spoken aloud.

The Remembering Room is an opportunity to slow down together. Through storytelling, photographs, handwritten notes, and creativity, each person contributes one page that becomes part of a shared memory book for the family.

There is no artistic ability required.
There is no right way to remember.

Simply come as you are.

The Remembering Room
Our time together

What to Expect

Our gathering will last approximately three hours.
During our time together we will —
Welcome everyone
Share the purpose of the gathering
Create individual memory pages
Tell stories as we create
Assemble the memory book together
Close with a time of gratitude and reflection
All art materials are provided.
No artistic experience is necessary.

What to Bring

If possible, please bring one or two of the following —
A favorite photograph
A funny story
A handwritten letter
A favorite saying
A recipe
A Bible verse (optional)
A favorite quote
Children’s artwork
A meaningful object
Ticket stubs and maps
Small memorabilia
Anything that tells a story
Don’t worry if you don’t have any of these.
Your memories are enough.

The Remembering Room
Before you begin

Reflection Questions

Choose one or two to inspire your page —
  1. What is your favorite memory?
  2. What made them laugh?
  3. What did they teach you?
  4. What will you always remember?
  5. What did they love?
  6. What words remind you of them?
  7. What is something you never want your family to forget?
  8. If they could speak to future generations, what might they say?
  9. What part of them lives on in you?

Preparing Your Home

A little preparation makes the room feel gracious.
Here is all you need —
  • One large table, if possible
  • Good lighting
  • Light snacks
  • Coffee or tea
  • Soft instrumental music
  • A nearby place for photos and keepsakes
  • Tissues
  • Enough chairs for everyone
We will take care of the rest.
